Line items are the core of your bid. They break the work into priced components your client can review.

## Add line items

<Steps>

1. Open your bid and go to the **Line Items** tab.

2. Click **Add Line Item**.

3. Enter a description, quantity, unit (each, LF, SF, and so on), and unit price.

4. The extended price calculates automatically.

</Steps>

## Organize with marks

Marks let you tag line items for organization and filtering. Common marks include:

- **Labor** — installation and crew time.
- **Material** — products and supplies.
- **Equipment** — rental or owned tool costs.

You can create custom marks that match how your company categorizes work.

## Use sublines

Break complex items into sublines for detailed pricing. A "Panel Installation" line item, for example, might have sublines for the panel, breakers, conduit, and wire. The parent line item totals up all of its sublines.

## Pull from the product catalog

Save time by pulling items from your product catalog.

<Steps>

1. Click **Add from Catalog** on a line item.

2. Search or browse your saved products.

3. Select an item — its description, unit, and price auto-fill.

</Steps>

![The Add from Catalog product picker](/docs-screenshots/bids/bid-add-from-catalog.png)

{/* SCREENSHOT NEEDED: the "Add from Catalog" product picker open over a bid, showing a product search and results. */}

<Aside type="tip">
  Keep your catalog current in the **Products** section so your team always
  bids with up-to-date pricing. This reduces errors and speeds up estimating.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="Role required">
  Managing the product catalog (creating and editing products) requires
  **Company Admin** or **Manager**. Other roles can still pull existing
  products into a bid.
</Aside>
