## Add a line item

<Steps>

1. **Open the Estimate tab.** Open your bid and click **Estimate**.

2. **Add a line.** In the **Add Line** form, enter a **Mark**, then optionally a **Product / System**, **Description**, and **Qty**. Click **Add Line**.

</Steps>

The **Mark** is a short identifier for the line, such as `A` or `W-1`. It is required and is how the line is labeled on the bid document -- it is not a markup percentage.

The line-items table shows these columns: **Mark**, **Product / System**, **Qty**, **Cost Items**, **Raw Cost**, **Sell Total**, and **Actions**.

<Aside type="tip">
  Pick an item from the **Product / System** menu to pull pricing from your [product catalog](/docs/products/catalog/). Keep the catalog current so every bid uses up-to-date numbers.
</Aside>

## Add cost items

Each line item expands into **Cost Items** -- the individual costs that make up its price.

<Steps>

1. **Expand the line.** Click the line to reveal its Cost Items sub-table.

2. **Add a cost.** Click **Add Cost** and choose a **Type**: **Material**, **Labor**, or **Other**.

3. **Enter the numbers.** Fill in **Description**, **Qty**, **Unit Cost**, and **UoM**. The **Extended** amount calculates automatically. Set **Buy Tax** and **Sell Tax** where they apply.

</Steps>

For a product set up as a kit, click **Import Kit** to pull its components in as cost items.

## Import from a spreadsheet

Use the CSV import button in the Estimate header to bring line items in from a spreadsheet instead of typing them.
