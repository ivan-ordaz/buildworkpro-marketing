The Gantt chart gives you a visual timeline of your project. It shows phases and tasks as horizontal bars, making it easy to see how work overlaps and where bottlenecks might occur.

## Viewing the Gantt chart

1. Open your project.
2. Click the **Gantt** tab.

The chart displays:

- **Phases** as grouped rows with summary bars.
- **Tasks** as individual bars within each phase.
- **Dependencies** as arrow lines connecting related tasks.
- **Today marker** as a vertical line so you can see where you stand.

## Adjusting timelines

You can update your schedule directly on the chart:

- **Drag the edges** of a task bar to extend or shorten its duration.
- **Drag the entire bar** to move a task to different dates.
- **Drag phase bars** to shift all tasks within that phase.

Changes save automatically and update the task dates everywhere in the app.

## Zoom and navigation

- Use the zoom controls to switch between day, week, and month views.
- Scroll horizontally to move through the timeline.
- Click on any bar to open the task detail panel.

## Tips

- Color-coded bars indicate task status so you can spot overdue items at a glance.
- If a task slips past its due date, the bar turns red.
- Use the Gantt chart in team meetings to review progress and adjust plans.
