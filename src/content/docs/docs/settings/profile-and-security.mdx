The **Profile** and **Security** tabs in Settings control your own account -- your details, your password, and how you verify sign-ins. They apply to you, not the whole organization, so every role can use them.

![Security settings tab showing the Password, Two-Factor Authentication, and Passkeys cards with the Set Up 2FA button](/docs-screenshots/settings/security-two-factor.png)

## Your profile

Open **Settings -> Profile** to update:

- **First Name** and **Last Name**.
- **Email** -- on password-based accounts, changing it requires your current password; the new address must then be verified.
- **Email Signature** -- appended to documents you email to customers (bids, change orders, invoices, and pay applications).

Your **Username** and **Role** are shown but cannot be changed here -- ask a Company Admin to change your role.

## Password

On **Settings -> Security**, the password card sends a reset link to your email rather than taking a new password inline. Click **Reset Password** (or **Set Password** if your account signs in with Google or Apple) and follow the link.

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a one-time code from an authenticator app on top of your password.

<Steps>

1. **Start setup.** On the Security tab, click **Set Up 2FA** and confirm your password.

2. **Scan the code.** Scan the QR code with an authenticator app such as Google Authenticator or Authy, or enter the key by hand.

3. **Verify.** Enter the 6-digit **Verification Code** and click **Verify and Enable**.

4. **Save your backup codes.** Copy or download the backup codes shown, then confirm you have saved them. Use them to sign in if you lose your authenticator.

</Steps>

Once enabled, a **2FA Active** badge appears. Click **Disable 2FA** (password plus a current code required) to turn it off.

<Aside type="caution">
  Store your backup codes somewhere safe. They are the only way back in if you lose access to your authenticator app.
</Aside>

## Passkeys

A passkey lets you sign in with Face ID, Touch ID, Windows Hello, or a hardware security key instead of a password.

<Steps>

1. **Add a passkey.** On the Security tab, click **Add Passkey** and optionally name it.

2. **Confirm.** Complete your device's prompt. If your account already has a password or 2FA, you re-authenticate first.

</Steps>

Each passkey can be renamed or removed. Passkeys backed up to your device's keychain show a **Synced** badge.

## Connected accounts

The **Connected Accounts** card links a Google or Apple account for one-click sign-in. You can link one social provider at a time, and you can only disconnect it if another sign-in method remains.
